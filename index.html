Camping

Mount San Jacinto has 2 developed campgrounds as well as 4 primitive wilderness campgrounds. Campers must have camping permits that are issued by Mount San Jacinto State Park. The permit for primitive camping is $5 per person.

Hiking

I have been to Mount San Jacinto a few times but only made it to the top once during my first visit. The journey was difficult. I encountered very cold weather close to the top of the peak and not to mention the many hours of constant walking over pieces of rocks and wood. The trail is pretty visible but the constant walk up is very exhausting. The 10 mile trail is considered to be one of the toughest and most beautiful hikes in Southern California. The hike takes an average of 6 hours to complete and is a popular area for hiking so expect to see other hikers as well. If you dont plan on hiking to the top there are other trails starting from long valley which range from easy to very streneous.

Winter Activities

Winters at Mount San Jacinto can vary depending on the season. Some winters produce several feet of snow while other seasons produce a dust or two. There are various snow-related activities that are available such as snowshoeing, sledding, skiing, and hiking. Make sure to contact the respective ranger station before performing any of these activities as well as filling out a wilderness day-use permit. It is also important to note that the ranger do not groom snowbound trails so be ready to navigate in your own with a map, compass, and GPS. Be sure to bring the necassary equipment as well such as microspikes, trekking poles, snowshoes, etc.
